Research Interests

01

My work sits in the qualitative theory of rational difference equations — discrete dynamical systems where a sequence's next term is a ratio of its own delayed past. I study the conditions under which such systems settle into a stable equilibrium, remain bounded, or fall into periodic cycles, particularly for higher-order systems with multiple delay terms.

My published work extends existing results to a general higher-order system with four delay parameters and eight positive real coefficients: deriving sufficient conditions for local stability through linearisation, proving global attractivity via monotone methods, establishing necessary and sufficient conditions for 2-cycle solutions based on the parity of the delay parameters, and validating results numerically in MATLAB. I'm currently looking toward fractional-order equations, stochastic perturbations, and time-varying coefficients — and their applications in mathematical biology, ecology, and economics.
